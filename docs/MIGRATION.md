# 🚚 Migration Guide: Moving to Hermes Clipper

Stop hoarding links in digital graveyards. Move your research into an active, agent-driven ecosystem.

---

## 💧 From Raindrop.io
Raindrop is excellent for storage, but it's a data silo. Here is how to feed your Raindrop collection to Hermes.

### Method A: The Agentic Import (Recommended)
1.  **Export as HTML:** In Raindrop, go to *Settings -> Import & Export -> Export to HTML*.
2.  **Dispatch Hermes:** 
    - Copy the exported list of URLs.
    - Ask Hermes: `"I am moving from Raindrop. Read these URLs, research them using the clipping skill, and file them into my Obsidian vault based on their topics."`
3.  **The Result:** Hermes will systematically visit each link, synthesize the content, and organize it intelligently.

### Method B: Manual Migration
Use the **Scout (Extension)** on your most critical Raindrop links to trigger a deep synthesis immediately.

---

## 📖 From Readwise / Reader
Readwise is great for highlights, but Hermes is better for **compounding intelligence.**

### The "Clean Sweep"
1.  **Export Markdown:** Use the Readwise "Obsidian Export" plugin if you already have it.
2.  **Refine with Hermes:** Once the files are in your vault (likely in a messy `Readwise/` folder), use the **Garden (Plugin)** ribbon icon on each note.
3.  **The Result:** Hermes will read the raw highlights, research the original source to fill in the gaps, and move the finalized "Second Brain" note to your `Research/` folder.
