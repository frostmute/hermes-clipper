# Hermes Clipper Manifesto

## The Problem
Humans are great at finding links, but terrible at filing them. We hoard tabs. We have folders named "To Read" that haven't been opened since 2022. Traditional clippers just move the mess from the browser to the vault.

## Why this? (The "Agent-Growth" Philosophy)
The world has enough webclippers. Most are **Data Silos**—digital graveyards where links go to die. 

**Hermes Clipper is different.**
You are already using Hermes for research, coding, and automation. Every disparate tool (Raindrop, Evernote, Pocket) you use is a "Leak" in your Agent's intelligence. When you clip to a silo, your Agent stays blind to that knowledge unless you manually import it with high friction.

### The Contextual Nutrient System
Hermes Clipper turns web-clipping into **Inherent Usage**. 
- **Zero Friction:** One click sends data into the vault Hermes already monitors.
- **Self-Improvement:** As Hermes synthesizes clips, it updates its own internal `MEMORY.md`. 
- **Intelligence Compounding:** Each clip isn't just a note; it's a "Nutrient" that makes Hermes sharper for your *next* task.

## Token Sovereignty vs. Efficiency
We admit it: Moving data through an LLM costs tokens. But we fight for **Signal over Noise**:
1. **Local Pre-Processing:** Our CLI strips the "Digital Fat" (HTML junk) before the Agent sees it.
2. **Caveman Compression:** 70% reduction in conversational overhead.
3. **The Synthesis Filter:** Hermes only "absorbs" and cross-links high-signal data. The rest stays in cold storage.

## Project Philosophy: "Sharp & Sarcastic"
- **Efficiency over Ceremony:** No bloated UIs. 
- **Agent-First:** If a human has to pick a folder, the system is broken.
- **Local Sovereignty:** Your vault, your keys, your agent.

## Deployment Strategy
- **Standard:** Localhost-only.
- **Power-User:** Bridge on a headless server + Tailscale.
- **Developer:** Custom templates and skill forks.
